Three findings, in order
01

The forecast

Across a held-out final month the weighted ensemble tracked the true global daily mean to 0.35 °C RMSE. The single best model, gradient boosting, reached 0.27 °C. Seven models were scored on the same untouched window, and none of them saw it during training.

02

The anomalies

Two independent detectors swept 150,000 hourly readings. A z-score test caught the statistical extremes; an isolation forest caught the structural oddities. The readings both methods flag are the ones worth trusting.

    The drivers

    The same pipeline turns to air quality. A gradient-boosted model of PM2.5 particulate pollution, read through SHAP over 1,000 samples, ranks what actually drives it. Humidity and visibility lead; temperature contributes less than most people expect.
